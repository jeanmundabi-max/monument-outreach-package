The challenge

Monument has product-market fit. It does not have a growth system.

Five billion pounds in deposits proves the product works. But the acquisition infrastructure that sustains this growth rate, and proves it to Nasdaq investors, does not exist yet.

01 · Single channel dependency

Deposit acquisition runs through best buy tables and rate competitiveness. When a competitor offers 10 basis points more, those customers leave. There is no brand moat, no content flywheel, and no channel diversification.

02 · No lifecycle marketing

Thousands of depositors reach fixed rate maturity with no renewal sequence, no cross-sell into property lending, and no onboarding journey. Every maturity date is a retention risk with no system managing it.

03 · No attribution or measurement

Customer acquisition cost is unknown. Conversion rate by step is unmeasured. Channel return on investment cannot be reported. When the board asks "how do we sustain this growth rate," the answer is a narrative, not a number.

04 · IPO without growth metrics

pre-IPO trajectory requires documented, predictable acquisition. Investor presentations need CAC by channel, LTV by segment, cohort retention at maturity, and growth rate projections. The function that produces these does not exist.

Beyond paid acquisition

The Authority Engine — Content That Compounds

Paid channels acquire depositors. The Authority Engine retains them and attracts new ones without spend.

1

LinkedIn Content Strategy

Weekly posts from Monument leadership targeting high earners, property investors, and professionals. Content about savings strategy for high earners, why FSCS matters above 85,000 pounds, the gap between high street and private banking. Not promotional. Educational. Builds trust before the deposit decision.

Content compounds. Paid stops the moment the budget stops.
2

Signal-Based Connection

Use LinkedIn signals to identify likely depositors: job title (Director and above, Partner, Founder), industry (law, finance, consulting, property), location (London, South East England), activity (engaging with savings and investment content). Connect with them. Let the content do the selling.

Every depositor Monument needs is identifiable before they visit the website
3

Email Nurture (20-Week)

Rate alerts, savings guides, market commentary. For people who visited the website but did not deposit. Value only, no hard sell. When they are ready, Monument is the name they remember.

95% of visitors are not ready today. Nurture captures the ones who will be ready in 3, 6, 12 months.
4

Retargeting Layer

5 to 10 pounds per day on Meta and LinkedIn retargeting website visitors. Keeps Monument visible between the first visit and the deposit decision, which can take weeks for 25,000 pound plus decisions.

Lowest cost channel. Highest intent audience. Already visited, just not ready yet.

This is what turns rate-driven depositors into brand-driven depositors. Brand depositors do not churn at maturity.
