Psychology Behind the Creative

Strategy Notes

01
Pain-Led

Name the Exact Situation

£250,000 is not a random number. It is specific enough to make the right person stop scrolling. The line "your bank pays you 1.5% and a call centre" is designed to trigger recognition, not outrage. The viewer thinks: "That is literally me." The ad does not sell a solution. It names a problem the viewer has accepted as normal. That reframing is the click.

Primary Emotion
Recognition → Indignation
Expected CTR Driver
Pattern interrupt through specificity
02
Aspiration

Reveal the Gap

This ad creates a category the viewer did not know existed. High street feels inadequate but private banking feels inaccessible. The ad names both extremes and says "someone filled it." The viewer's curiosity is not about what the product is. It is about what they have been missing. The question mark in the gap visual is literal: "There is something here you do not know about."

Primary Emotion
Curiosity → Aspiration
Expected CTR Driver
Zeigarnik effect (open loop)
03
Education

Category Creation

"Monzo and Coutts" are brands every UK saver recognises. Placing them as bookends creates an instant mental map. "Something in between" is the insight: the viewer realises there is a third option they were never offered. The Trustpilot badge appears here because education requires proof. Thousands of others already found this. You are late, not early. That social proof converts hesitation into FOMO.

Primary Emotion
Discovery → FOMO
Expected CTR Driver
Category framing + social proof
