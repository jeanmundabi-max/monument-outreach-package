The Depositors You Are Missing
and How I Would Reach Them

Monument has £7 billion in deposits and 1,794 Trustpilot reviews at 4.7 stars. The product earns loyalty once people find it. The question is how they find it.

The Pattern

Right now, the answer is rate tables. Moneyfacts. Best buy comparison sites. Depositors arrive because Monument has a competitive number. They stay or leave based on whether someone else posts a better number next quarter.

The depositors Monument is missing are the ones who never check Moneyfacts. High earners who keep £250,000 in a high street account paying 1.5% because they have never heard of an alternative. Property investors who need savings alongside lending but do not know Monument offers both. Professionals who would choose a bank with a personal relationship manager if anyone told them it existed for less than £1 million.

These people are identifiable. They are on LinkedIn right now. Nobody is reaching them.
Who They Are
1

Directors, Partners, and Founders

Earning £100,000 plus. Their LinkedIn profile shows the job title. Their savings capacity is a function of income. Monument's personal service proposition is built for them. They just do not know it.

Signal: Job title on LinkedIn
2

Property Investors

Findable through Companies House director filings and LinkedIn activity. They need savings alongside lending. Monument offers both. No competitor markets this combination to this segment.

Signal: Companies House + LinkedIn
3

Professionals in Law, Finance, and Medicine

High earning industries with predictable savings accumulation. Identifiable by industry filter on LinkedIn. First bank to reach them with relevant content wins the deposit.

Signal: Industry filter on LinkedIn
4

Recently Promoted Senior Professionals

LinkedIn job change signals indicate equity events, bonuses, or salary increases. New money looking for a home. Monument's 4.09% AER and personal manager are compelling at exactly the moment they have cash to deploy.

Signal: Job change on LinkedIn

Rate depositors churn at maturity. They arrived for the number. When the number changes, they leave. No emotional connection. Rate tables attract price buyers, not brand buyers. Price buyers compare. Brand buyers trust. Brand buyers renew at maturity, cross sell into lending, and refer friends.

Monument's relationship manager model is built for brand buyers. Rate tables keep delivering price buyers. Before an IPO, every growth channel needs documented cost per acquisition, lifetime value, and cohort retention. "We have the best rate" is not a system. It cannot be attributed, segmented, or optimised.

What I Would Build
1

Comparison Content and Paid Acquisition

"Monument vs Coutts" and "Monument vs HSBC Premier" pages. Google Ads on "best savings rates UK." LinkedIn Ads targeting high earners by job title, industry, and location. Measurable depositor leads from non rate table sources within 30 days.

Weeks 1 to 4
2

Lifecycle Marketing

Maturity renewal sequences at 60, 30, and 14 days. Cross sell into property lending. Depositor onboarding. Rate alert capture. Turns rate depositors into relationship depositors and stops silent churn at maturity.

Weeks 3 to 8
3

The Authority Engine

Weekly LinkedIn content from Monument leadership. Not promotional — educational. "Why FSCS protection matters above £85,000." "The gap between your high street bank and private banking." Signal-based connections with people whose profiles indicate savings capacity. Email nurture for the 95% not ready to deposit today. Micro budget retargeting keeping Monument visible between first visit and deposit decision. This is what creates brand depositors. It compounds. It does not depend on rate competitiveness.
