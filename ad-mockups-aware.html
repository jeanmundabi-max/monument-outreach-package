Psychology Behind the Creative

Strategy Notes

01
Comparison

Coutts vs Monument

Anchoring bias. The viewer sees Coutts' million-pound barrier first. Monument's £25,000 entry feels like a bargain by contrast. This is not about price. It is about making private banking feel accessible. The implicit message: you deserve this level of service, and someone finally removed the gate.

Primary Emotion
Frustration → Relief
Expected CTR Driver
Curiosity gap + price anchor
02
Social Proof

Trust Stack

Three proof points in rapid succession: volume (£7B), peer validation (Trustpilot 4.7), and authority (Best Savings Provider). Each addresses a different objection. The volume says "this is real." The stars say "people like you approve." The award says "experts agree." Stacking removes the need for the viewer to verify individually.

Primary Emotion
Uncertainty → Confidence
Expected CTR Driver
Social validation cascade
03
Rate-Led

Lead With the Number

For the Aware audience comparing savings accounts, the rate is the conversation opener. 4.09% is the hook. But the differentiator is the line beneath: "Your relationship manager calls within 24 hours." Every competitor can quote a rate. Nobody else promises a named human on the phone tomorrow. The rate gets the click. The relationship manager gets the account.

Primary Emotion
Comparison → Decision
Expected CTR Driver
Concrete number + service gap
